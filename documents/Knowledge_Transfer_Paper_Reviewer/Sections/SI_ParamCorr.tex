\label{SI:param_corr}
Figure~\ref{fig:param_corr} and Table~\ref{tab:param_corr} report the posterior parameter correlation matrices computed from the final parameter ensemble across all six datasets. Of the 276 pairwise combinations per dataset, only the $Lac_{\max1}$ and $Lac_{\max2}$ pair exceeds the $|r|>0.7$ threshold. This occurs in three datasets, including Cell Line A shake flask, Cell Line A bioreactor 36.5\,°C, and Cell Line B Feed C.

The two parameters govern the lactate-specific rate:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d(V[\mathrm{Lac}])}{dt} = q_{\mathrm{Lac}}\,V X_v - F_{\mathrm{out}}[\mathrm{Lac}]
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    q_{\mathrm{Lac}} = \left(\frac{\mu}{Y_{X/Lac}} - Y_{Lac/Glc}\,q_{Glc}\right)\frac{Lac_{\max1} - [\mathrm{Lac}]}{Lac_{\max1}} + m_{Lac}\,\frac{Lac_{\max2} - [\mathrm{Lac}]}{Lac_{\max2}}
\end{equation}
\noindent where $[\mathrm{Lac}]$ (mM) is the extracellular lactate concentration, $q_{\mathrm{Lac}}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the specific lactate rate, $X_v$ (cell\,L$^{-1}$) is the viable cell density, $F_{\mathrm{out}}$ (L\,h$^{-1}$) is the sampling flow rate, $\mu$ (h$^{-1}$) is the specific growth rate, $Y_{X/Lac}$ (cell\,mmol$^{-1}$) is the yield of biomass on lactate, $Y_{Lac/Glc}$ (mmol\,mmol$^{-1}$) is the yield of lactate from glucose through glycolysis and pyruvate synthesis, $q_{Glc}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the specific glucose consumption rate, $Lac_{\max1}$ and $Lac_{\max2}$ (mM) are the activation constants for lactate consumption with nominal values of 21.20 and 16.00\,mM respectively, and $m_{Lac}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the maintenance coefficient for lactate participation in other metabolic pathways.

The first term represents the growth-associated flux modulated by $(Lac_{\max1} - [\mathrm{Lac}])/Lac_{\max1}$. The second represents the maintenance flux modulated by $(Lac_{\max2} - [\mathrm{Lac}])/Lac_{\max2}$ and scaled by $m_{Lac}$. Because the growth-associated term depends on the time-varying specific growth rate $\mu$ while the maintenance term depends on the constant rate $m_{Lac}$, the two parameters are structurally identifiable in principle. However, when $[\mathrm{Lac}]$ remains well below both thresholds the two saturation factors scale proportionally and opposing shifts in $Lac_{\max1}$ and $Lac_{\max2}$ can produce near-identical net $q_{\mathrm{Lac}}$ trajectories. This parameter compensation gives rise to a strong negative posterior correlation.

Whether this compensation is resolved depends on the dynamic range of $[\mathrm{Lac}]$. In net-accumulation datasets (Cell Line A shake flask, $r=-0.740$; Cell Line A bioreactor 36.5\,°C, $r=-0.750$) lactate evolves gradually and the two saturation terms remain jointly compensable. Cell Line B Feed C ($r=-0.733$) shows eventual net consumption but the transition is sufficiently delayed for the compensation to persist.
