\label{SI:param_corr}
{\color{blue}
Figure~\ref{fig:param_corr} shows the full posterior parameter correlation matrices for all six datasets, computed from the final parameter ensemble at the last assimilation step of the long-term prediction run (Supplementary Information~\ref{long-term}). Table~\ref{tab:param_corr} summarises all pairs exceeding $|r|>0.7$,. Only $Lac_{\max 1}$ and $Lac_{\max 2}$ exceed this threshold, and only in the three Cell Line A and Feed C datasets where lactate accumulation occurs; the remaining datasets show no strongly correlated pairs. The negative sign reflects the opposing kinetic roles of the two parameters in the lactate switch: as one activation constant rises, the other falls. This structural correlation does not affect the reliability of state estimation but indicates that the two parameters cannot be independently identified from data alone.
