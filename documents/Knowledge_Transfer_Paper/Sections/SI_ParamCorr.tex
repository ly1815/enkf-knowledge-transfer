\label{SI:param_corr}
Figure~\ref{fig:param_corr} and Table~\ref{tab:param_corr} report the posterior parameter correlation matrices computed from the final parameter ensemble across all six datasets. Of the 276 pairwise combinations per dataset, only $Lac_{\max1}$--$Lac_{\max2}$ exceeds the $|r|>0.7$ threshold, in three datasets.

The two parameters govern the lactate-specific rate:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{d(V[\mathrm{Lac}])}{dt} = q_{\mathrm{Lac}}\,V X_v - F_{\mathrm{out}}[\mathrm{Lac}]
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    q_{\mathrm{Lac}} = \left(\frac{\mu}{Y_{X/Lac}} - Y_{Lac/Glc}\,q_{Glc}\right)\frac{Lac_{\max1} - [\mathrm{Lac}]}{Lac_{\max1}} + m_{Lac}\,\frac{Lac_{\max2} - [\mathrm{Lac}]}{Lac_{\max2}}
\end{equation}
\noindent where $[\mathrm{Lac}]$ (mM) is the extracellular lactate concentration, $q_{\mathrm{Lac}}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the specific lactate rate, $X_v$ (cell\,L$^{-1}$) is the viable cell density, $F_{\mathrm{out}}$ (L\,h$^{-1}$) is the sampling flow rate, $\mu$ (h$^{-1}$) is the specific growth rate, $Y_{X/Lac}$ (cell\,mmol$^{-1}$) is the yield of biomass on lactate, $Y_{Lac/Glc}$ (mmol\,mmol$^{-1}$) is the yield of lactate from glucose through glycolysis and pyruvate synthesis, $q_{Glc}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the specific glucose consumption rate, $Lac_{\max1}$ and $Lac_{\max2}$ (mM) are the activation constants for lactate consumption with nominal values of 21.20 and 16.00\,mM respectively, and $m_{Lac}$ (mmol\,cell$^{-1}$\,h$^{-1}$) is the maintenance coefficient for lactate participation in other metabolic pathways.

The first term represents the growth-associated flux, modulated by $(Lac_{\max1} - [\mathrm{Lac}])/Lac_{\max1}$; the second represents the maintenance flux, modulated by $(Lac_{\max2} - [\mathrm{Lac}])/Lac_{\max2}$ and scaled by $m_{Lac}$. Although the two terms are driven by functionally distinct quantities, making $Lac_{\max1}$ and $Lac_{\max2}$ structurally identifiable in principle, their saturation factors respond similarly when $[\mathrm{Lac}]$ remains well below both thresholds. A compensating shift — higher $Lac_{\max1}$ reducing suppression of the growth term, lower $Lac_{\max2}$ accelerating the maintenance sign change — can produce near-identical net $q_{\mathrm{Lac}}$ trajectories, giving rise to a strong negative posterior correlation.

Whether this degeneracy persists depends on the dynamic range of $[\mathrm{Lac}]$. In net-accumulation datasets (Cell Line A shake flask, $r=-0.740$; Cell Line A bioreactor 36.5\,°C, $r=-0.750$), lactate evolves gradually and the two saturation terms remain jointly compensable. In datasets with steep net consumption (32\,°C bioreactor, Feed U, Feed U plus 40\%), $[\mathrm{Lac}]$ traverses a wider range, the saturation factors diverge, and the correlation drops below 0.7. Cell Line B Feed C ($r=-0.733$) shows eventual net consumption, but the transition is sufficiently delayed that the compensation persists.

Despite the correlation, the EnKF posterior means for Cell Line B Feed C shift in the same direction as — and are comparable in magnitude to — the values obtained from bespoke GS46 reparametrisation by Kotidis \textit{et al.}~\cite{Kotidis2019UseCultures} (Table~\ref{tab:lacmax_comparison}). Both values decrease from the Cell Line A prior, consistent with Cell Line B exhibiting earlier onset of net lactate consumption. This directional agreement indicates that the observed lactate dynamics constrain the net effect of the two parameters on $q_{\mathrm{Lac}}$, even when their individual contributions cannot be fully separated. Since state estimation depends on this net effect rather than on individual parameter values, it remains robust to the correlation.
