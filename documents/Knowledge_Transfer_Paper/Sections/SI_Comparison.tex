{\color{blue}
Table~\ref{tab:param_overlap} compares the ten most-converging EnKF parameters (ranked by ensemble spread reduction across all six datasets) with the ten parameters identified by global sensitivity analysis (GSA) and subsequently re-estimated by Kotidis \textit{et al.}~\cite{Kotidis2019UseCultures} for Cell Line B. Seven of the ten parameters overlap, despite the EnKF receiving no prior sensitivity information — it identifies the dominant parameter subspace purely from sequential data assimilation. Both methods flag the core growth ($\mu_{\text{max}}$), lactate switching ($Lac_{\text{max1}}$, $Lac_{\text{max2}}$), and amino acid yield ($Y_{X,\text{Asn}}$, $Y_{X,\text{Glu}}$, $Y_{X,\text{Amm}}$) parameters as requiring adaptation for cross-cell-line transfer. Each method additionally identifies one mAb-production parameter, though a different one: $m_{\text{mAb}}$ (maintenance-associated) via GSA versus $Y_{\text{mAb},\mu}$ (growth-associated) via EnKF. The three EnKF-only parameters ($Y_{X,\text{Lac}}$, $Y_{\text{mAb},\mu}$, $K_{\text{Asn}}$) relate to product formation and substrate affinity — sensitivities that may emerge from the time-resolved nature of the EnKF update but are less visible to a variance-based GSA evaluated at steady state. Conversely, the GSA-only parameters ($Y_{X,\text{Glc}}$, $Y_{X,\text{Gln}}$) reflect global input--output sensitivity that the EnKF, constrained by the specific measurement trajectory, does not rank as highly. This 70\% agreement supports the claim that the EnKF can largely bypass the traditional GSA--reparametrisation workflow while arriving at a consistent set of influential parameters.
